## Current openings

### 1) Volunteer video editor (5–10 hrs/week)

**Purpose:** Produce compelling video content that supports campaigns, explains key ideas and highlights events/spokespersons

**Basic terms of reference**

- Edit short-form videos (15–90s) for social platforms, including captions and basic motion graphics.
- Edit occasional longer pieces (interviews, talks, explainers, event footage).
- Create reusable templates (intro/outro, lower thirds and caption styles) aligned with our brand.
- Manage raw footage, drafts, versioning and delivery timelines.
- Ensure accessibility and clarity (captions, pacing and audio levelling).

**Skills and requirements**

- Proficiency with video software.
- A track record of producing videos.
- Comfortable iterating quickly from feedback.

**What you'll get**

- A meaningful role working on the most salient and urgent issue of our time
- A supportive team, clear priorities and ownership of real work
- Experience in advocacy comms and digital campaigning
- Reference letter on request

**How to apply**

Send an email to [vacancies@pauseai.info](mailto:vacancies@pauseai.info) with the following:

1. In the subject state which role you're applying for
2. Add a short note on why you're a good fit (1 paragraph max)
3. Attach your CV
4. Share three examples of your work
5. Tell us your time zone, how many hours you can dedicate per week and when you can start

_We review applicants on a rolling basis and reserve the right to close the call for applications early_

### 2) Volunteer grant prospecting researcher (5–10 hrs/month)

**Purpose:** Research foundations and other funders to build a pipeline of grant opportunities aligned with PauseAI's mission in AI safety, technology policy, and civic advocacy.

**Basic terms of reference**

- Search grant databases (Candid, Instrumentl, government portals) for relevant opportunities.
- Evaluate funders for mission fit, eligibility, grant size, and deadlines.
- Maintain a shared grant tracker and summarise funder priorities.
- Flag high-priority opportunities with sufficient lead time.

**Skills and requirements**

- Detail-oriented and self-directed with strong research skills.
- Interest in AI safety.
- Prior fundraising or nonprofit experience is a plus; willingness to learn grant databases is enough to start.

**What you'll get**

- Direct mission impact working on the most urgent issue of our time
- Fundraising experience in the AI safety space
- A supportive team and ownership of real work
- Reference letter on request

**How to apply**

Send an email to [vacancies@pauseai.info](mailto:vacancies@pauseai.info) with the following:

1. In the subject state which role you're applying for
2. Add a short note on why you're a good fit (1 paragraph max)
3. Attach your CV
4. Tell us your time zone, how many hours you can dedicate per month and when you can start

_We review applicants on a rolling basis and reserve the right to close the call for applications early_
